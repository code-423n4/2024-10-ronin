Hello - what's the rationale for making pool functions permissioned? Is the idea to authorize only the operation contracts to interact with the pool?
doublevkay 10/19/2024 01:58
Hi, there are some things I would like to talk about the Governance feature. The governance features are primarily designed to support the initialization of liquidity in V2. This is intended to help prevent chaos from arbitrary swaps and mints when initializing pairs/pools. Additionally, these features open a use case where pools can only be interactable (for minting and swapping) once certain conditions are met - such as liquidity being available and any mistakes being checked. Governance features have also been integrated into V3. It‚Äôs important to note that both V2 and V3 are built on top of the Uniswap foundation, which is expected to operate effectively without governance, ensuring that users do not incur losses even if the permissioned features are bypassed. We also have a vision of removing the entire governance feature in the future to make Katana fully permissionless. These are parts of the reason behind the severity downgrading of the Governance contract.
All vulnerabilities in the KatanaGovernance contract that do not affect user funds will have their severity downgraded by one level.
